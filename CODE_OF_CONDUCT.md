# OpenMedVisionX Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We are committed to a welcoming, safe, and respectful community for students,
educators, researchers, clinicians, developers, and contributors of every
background and experience level.

## Expected behavior

Community members should:

- communicate with empathy, patience, and technical honesty;
- welcome questions and explain unfamiliar concepts without condescension;
- give specific, actionable feedback and assume good intent;
- respect privacy, consent, intellectual property, and model/data licenses;
- acknowledge uncertainty, especially around medical and AI results;
- keep discussions focused on education, research, and project improvement.

## Unacceptable behavior

The following are not acceptable:

- harassment, discrimination, threats, sexualized conduct, or personal attacks;
- publishing another person's private information;
- sharing patient data, credentials, private model artifacts, or confidential
  incident details in public project channels;
- presenting project output as a clinical diagnosis or encouraging unsafe
  medical use;
- sustained disruption, intimidation, or retaliation after a report.

## Reporting and enforcement

Report conduct concerns privately to the repository maintainers through an
available private project channel. Security vulnerabilities, exposed secrets,
or patient-data incidents should follow [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md), not a
public issue.

Maintainers will review reports in good faith, protect reporter privacy as far
as practical, and may edit or remove content, issue a warning, temporarily
restrict participation, or permanently exclude a participant. Retaliation
against a reporter is prohibited.

This code applies in project spaces and when someone officially represents the
project in another venue.

## Attribution

This policy is informed by the Contributor Covenant and common open-source
community standards. It may be updated as the project and its reporting
channels mature.
